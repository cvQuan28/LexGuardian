# User Personas

LexGuardian serves three distinct user groups, each with different goals, mental models, and tolerance for complexity.

---

## Persona 1: Linh — The Contract Lawyer

**Role:** Associate at a mid-size law firm in Ho Chi Minh City  
**Experience:** 4 years of legal practice  
**Daily tools:** MS Word, email, paper contracts, Google search  
**AI experience:** Has tried ChatGPT, wary of hallucinations

**Goals:**
- Review vendor contracts quickly and find what could hurt her client
- Verify that legal references in a contract are still in effect
- Draft clause suggestions to redline back to counterparties
- Cite sources confidently to supervisors and clients

**Pain points:**
- Reading 60-page contracts looking for 3 problematic clauses
- Googling Vietnamese laws and not knowing if results are current
- Can't trust AI answers because they're not cited
- Too much time on routine review, not enough on strategy

**Key insight:** Linh doesn't trust AI. She needs proof. Every statement must have a source she can verify in one click. The moment the system shows a citation and she can jump to the exact page, she converts.

**What she does NOT want:**
- To configure AI settings or choose retrieval modes
- To see chunk counts, vector dimensions, or model names
- To learn a new complicated system
- To get an answer she can't show to her supervisor

**LexGuardian promise for Linh:** "Drop the contract, get the risks, verify every claim."

---

## Persona 2: Minh — The Business Operations Manager

**Role:** Operations lead at a 200-person tech company  
**Experience:** Business background, no legal training  
**Daily tools:** Notion, Slack, Google Drive, occasional DocuSign  
**AI experience:** Uses ChatGPT regularly, comfortable with it

**Goals:**
- Understand what he's signing without calling a lawyer every time
- Run standard vendor contracts against company risk playbooks
- Know if a specific policy or regulation has changed
- "Translate" legal language into plain business English

**Pain points:**
- Vendor agreements written in dense Vietnamese legalese he can't parse
- Doesn't know which clauses are negotiable
- Calls the company lawyer for routine questions, feels bad about wasting their time
- Regulations change; he doesn't know when he's operating on old information

**Key insight:** Minh wants answers in plain language. He's comfortable with AI. His blocker is trust — he needs to know the AI isn't making things up. The citation panel that opens to the exact source is his trust signal.

**What he does NOT want:**
- Legal jargon in the answer text
- To be told "I am not a lawyer, please consult one" without any actual help
- Multiple steps to get to an answer

**LexGuardian promise for Minh:** "Understand what you're signing. Know what changed. Move faster."

---

## Persona 3: Hoa — The In-House Compliance Officer

**Role:** Compliance at a financial services firm  
**Experience:** 8 years, deep regulatory knowledge  
**Daily tools:** Internal compliance databases, government portals, Excel  
**AI experience:** Skeptical, has seen AI tools fail on regulatory nuance

**Goals:**
- Monitor regulatory changes across relevant Vietnamese laws
- Verify that internal policies align with current regulations
- Run quick searches on specific legal provisions
- Build an auditable record of compliance decisions

**Pain points:**
- Regulatory landscape changes frequently; keeping up is exhausting
- Current tools require manual cross-referencing across multiple sources
- Needs exact citations for everything — regulators don't accept "AI said so"
- Internal policies drift out of compliance without being noticed

**Key insight:** Hoa is the most demanding user. She will break the system with edge cases, ambiguous statutes, and overlapping regulations. She needs the Knowledge Graph / Explore mode when tracking complex entity relationships across laws. She needs every answer to have a canonical citation she can put in an audit document.

**What she does NOT want:**
- Confident answers on edge cases where the law is genuinely ambiguous
- The system to hide uncertainty — she prefers "insufficient information" to a confident wrong answer
- Any delay between finding a risk and understanding its implication

**LexGuardian promise for Hoa:** "Cite-able answers. Auditable decisions. Proactive regulatory monitoring."

## Secondary Personas

**HR Manager (Phuong):** Uses LexGuardian to check employment contract compliance, understand termination procedures. Needs plain-language answers. Uses Ask flow only.

**External Auditor (Nam):** Reviews large document sets against regulatory checklists. Needs bulk document analysis. Heavy Library + Analyze user.

---

## What Unites All Three

1. They all need **citations** — no source, no trust
2. They all need **plain-language explanations** alongside legal text
3. They all need **speed** — they're using LexGuardian because manual review is too slow
4. None of them care about **how** the AI works, only **whether it's right**
